Verbal Mini-Demo

Explained the restaurant analogy to my partner in breakout rooms. I said: you're at a restaurant. You don't go cook your own food. You look at the menu (the API), tell the waiter (HTTP) what you want (a request), and the kitchen (the server) makes it and sends it back (the response). If they're out of something, the waiter tells you "we don't have that" -- that's a 404. If the kitchen is literally on fire, that's a 500. JSON is the plate the food arrives on -- it's the format, the way the data is organized so both sides can understand it.

Partner asked: "What's the difference between GET and POST in restaurant terms?" I said: GET is looking at the menu or asking "what soups do you have today?" -- you're just reading, not changing anything. POST is placing an order -- you're creating something new. The kitchen didn't have "Penn's pasta order" before, and now it does.

Exit Ticket

The thing that clicked today: APIs aren't magic. They're just URLs that return data instead of web pages. I already use them without thinking about it every time I load a page. The restaurant analogy is corny but it actually works, especially the error codes part. 404 = "we don't serve that" is going to stick.
