Feature Prioritization

What's essential, what's nice, what can wait.

The Buckets

Must Have (MVP) Nice to Have Later
AI Q&A chat (plain language, no jargon) Case worker dashboard Offline mode (downloadable guides)
Step-by-step walkthrough modules Module assignment system Multi-language support
Progress tracking (resume where you left off) Custom local resource guides SMS notifications / reminders
Built-in glossary sidebar Integration with reentry services APIs

Why these three

AI Q&A: This is the thing that makes the tool different from a static tutorial site. Someone can type "how do I make a gmail" and get a real, patient, step-by-step answer in language they understand. Without this the app is just another FAQ page.

Walkthrough modules: The Q&A handles questions, but people also need structured guidance for the big tasks. Setting up email. Using a smartphone. Filling out an online job application. These need to be pre-built, tested, and clear.

Progress tracking: People in reentry are juggling a hundred things. They might use this tool for 10 minutes at the library, then come back two days later. They need to know what they've done and where they left off without starting over.
