Comp Research

What already exists, what's broken about it, and where I fit in.

Prompt 1: Existing Solutions

Asked ChatGPT: "What apps, websites, or tools currently help people returning from incarceration learn to use modern technology? List the top 5."

What came back

1. GCFGlobal (GCFLearnFree.org)

Free digital literacy courses. Covers everything from "what is a computer" to Excel. General audience. No reentry context.

2. Northstar Digital Literacy

Assessment-based. Libraries and workforce centers use it to test and certify basic digital skills. Standardized, not personalized.

3. DigitalLearn.org (PLA)

Public Library Association's tool. Short video courses on things like "getting started with a tablet." Aimed at older adults and digital newcomers. No AI, no interactivity.

4. Reentry-specific programs (Defy Ventures, The Last Mile)

These teach entrepreneurship and coding inside prisons. Great, but they're in-prison programs, not post-release tools. And they focus on career skills, not "how do I use this phone."

5. General AI chatbots (ChatGPT, etc.)

Technically anyone can ask ChatGPT how to set up email. But the responses assume baseline digital fluency. The interface itself requires comfort with tech. And there's no structure, no progress, no curriculum.

Prompt 2: Gaps

Asked: "For each of those tools, what do users commonly complain about? What's missing?"

Tool Does Well Missing
GCFGlobal Broad coverage, free, well-organized Generic. No awareness of reentry context. No AI. No personalization.
Northstar Standardized assessments, library adoption Test-focused, not learning-focused. Tells you what you don't know, doesn't teach it.
DigitalLearn Simple video format, public library backing Passive. No interaction. No way to ask questions. No progress tracking.
Reentry programs Understand the population In-prison only. Career-focused. Not available post-release for basic digital tasks.
ChatGPT Can answer anything, available 24/7 No structure. Assumes fluency. Intimidating UI. No progress. No curriculum.

Prompt 3: My Angle

Asked: "I'm building an AI-powered tool that helps returning citizens learn basic technology with plain-language Q&A, guided walkthroughs, and progress tracking. What makes this different?"

▶ My opportunity Nobody combines AI-powered conversational help with structured learning designed specifically for people who missed years of tech evolution. The existing tools are either too generic (GCFGlobal), too passive (DigitalLearn), too assessment-focused (Northstar), or too unstructured (ChatGPT). My app sits in the gap: AI that's patient and jargon-free, walkthroughs built for the reentry experience, and progress that persists so people can learn in the small windows of time they actually have.

Hallucination check

Verified: GCFGlobal, Northstar, DigitalLearn, Defy Ventures, and The Last Mile are all real. ChatGPT didn't invent any of them. I also searched for "digital literacy app for formerly incarcerated" and didn't find a direct competitor doing what I'm proposing.
